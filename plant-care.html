Plant Care Tips

The Golden Rule: Watering

Overwatering is the most common mistake new plant parents make. It's better to under-water than over-water. A simple test is to stick your finger about an inch into the soil. If it feels dry, it's time to water. If it's moist, wait a few more days. Most common houseplants prefer their soil to dry out slightly between waterings.

Let There Be Light

Light is food for plants. Most houseplants thrive in bright, indirect light. This means a spot near a window where the sun's rays don't directly hit the leaves, which can cause them to burn. A sheer curtain can be a great way to diffuse bright sunlight. Low-light plants like Snake Plants and ZZ Plants can tolerate shadier corners.

Common Problems & Solutions

Yellow Leaves: This is often a sign of overwatering. Check the soil moisture and allow it to dry out before watering again. It can also sometimes mean a nutrient deficiency.

Brown, Crispy Tips: This usually indicates dry air. Try misting your plants with water every few days or placing them near a humidifier. It can also be a sign of inconsistent watering.

Pests: Small insects like spider mites or mealybugs can occasionally appear. The best first step is to wipe the leaves with a damp cloth and a mild soap solution. Neem oil is a great natural pesticide for more persistent issues.
